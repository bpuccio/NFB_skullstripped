The Neurofeedback Skull-stripped (NFBS) repository is a database of 125 T1-weighted anatomical MRI scans that are manually skull-stripped. NFBS provides researchers with gold standard training and testing data for developing machine learning algorithms. The data was collected as a part of the Enhanced Rockland Sample Neurofeedback Study. NFBS is available for download on the NFB_skullstripped Github page.

For more information, please see:

Puccio et al. The Preprocessed Connectomes Project: repository of manually-corrected skull-stripped T1-weighted anatomical MRI data. (2016).

***Table of Contents*** * [Abstract](#abstract) * [Contents of the Repository](#contents-of-the-repository) * [Data Analysis](#data-analysis) * [Downloading the Repository](#downloading-the-repository) * [Contributors](##contributors) * [References](##references) ## Abstract Skull-stripping, also known as brain extraction, is the procedure of removing non-brain tissue from anatomical MRI data. This procedure is necessary for calculating brain volume and for improving the quality of brain image registration and segmentation. Developing new brain extraction algorithms and evaluating their performance requires gold standard} data from a variety of different scanners and acquisition methods. We complement existing repositories with manually-corrected brain extractions for 125 T1-weighted anatomical scans from the Enhanced Rockland Sample Neurofeedback Study. Skull-stripped images were obtained using an iterative two-step procedure. In the first step, the BEaST algorithm was used to automatically obtain brain masks. Following visual inspection, the BEaST-derived masks were manually edited[^1]. In total, 85 of the skull-strips were hand-edited and 40 were deemed to not need editing. The resulting repository was used to compare the outputs from the BET[^2], 3dSkullStrip[^3], and FreeSurfer HWA[^4] algorithms (BEaST was not compared to avoid bias). Two outside repositories, IBSR[^5] and LPBA40[^6], were also tested with these algorithms, as well as with BEaST using our repository in its prior library. ## Contents of the Repository The repository contains data from 125 participants, ranging from 21 to 45 years old. For each participant, the repository contains: * Structural T1-weighted anonymized (de-faced) image * Skull-stripped image * Brain mask The structural MRI data was collected as a part of the Neurofeedback study in the Enhanced Nathan Kline Institute - Rockland Sample. The resolution of the images is 1mm3 and each file is in NiFTI format (.nii.gz). ## Data Analysis
